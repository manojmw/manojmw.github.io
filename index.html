About

Manoj M Wagle

Doctoral Researcher

The University of Sydney

Children's Medical Research Institute

I am a first-year PhD candidate at the School of Mathematics and Statistics in the Faculty of Science at The University of Sydney and the Computational Systems Biology Group at the Children's Medical Research Institute, supervised by A/Prof Pengyi Yang.

I am highly fascinated by the interdisciplinary potential of Bioinformatics and Data Science to advance our understanding of complex cellular activities and uncover hitherto unknown mechanisms of disease pathogenesis. My current research focuses on developing and applying novel computational approaches, particularly leveraging Artificial Intelligence/Deep Learning-based techniques to facilitate the analysis of diverse high-throughput biological data. Specifically, I am interested in applying these approaches to answer questions in the field of Single-cell omics, Cell identity & Cell-fate decisions, and Stem cell research.

When I'm not working, I love spending time with my dogs, hiking, practicing yoga/meditation, or just experimenting in the kitchen :) 


Research Interests:

  • Bioinformatics/Computational Biology
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Deep Learning
  • Cell Fate
  • Cell Identity
  • Stem Cells
