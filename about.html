PhD Candidate

The University of Sydney

I am a PhD candidate in the Faculty of Science at The University of Sydney and the Children's Medical Research Institute. I conduct my research in the School of Mathematics and Statistics (USYD) and Kellis Lab (Massachusetts Institute of Technology).

I am fascinated by the interdisciplinary potential of Biology and Artificial Intelligence to improve our understanding of complex cellular activities underlying health and disease. My current research focuses on developing and applying novel computational approaches, particularly leveraging interpretable deep learning techniques to facilitate the analysis of heterogeneous biological data. Specifically, I am interested in applying these approaches to answer questions in the field of Biomedical AI, Neuroscience and Precision Medicine.

Outside of research, I love spending time in nature and with my dogs, hiking, practicing mindfulness, Shōtōkan martial arts, badminton or simply experimenting in the kitchen!!
